Why Meta.

In 2015, I was at Adweek in New York when I heard Meta's marketing chief say something that stuck with me for over a decade: "Do good work for good people." That's not marketing. It's a standard. And it's the standard I've tried to hold myself to in every build I've shipped.

I'm drawn to Meta's technical ambition and its experience building products at extraordinary scale. I want to work on AI systems where performance must translate into reliable, useful experiences for a global and diverse user base — the billions, not the hundreds. That's a different engineering problem than what most of the industry is working on.

My production work is built on a hybrid AI architecture: LLMs handle what they're good at — reading messy input, drafting, classification — while deterministic, unit-tested code handles anything consequential. I've shipped a two-channel AI inbound system that attributed $9M+ in pipeline, a compliance screening engine with zero production errors, and a post-call automation pipeline I rebuilt from 0% pass rate.

The role I want at Meta is Forward Deployed Engineer — helping large enterprise customers deploy AI products effectively, and closing the loop between real-world usage and product development. My background combines 15 years of enterprise sales with deep hands-on AI build practice.

Do good work for good people. That's still the standard. Meta is where I want to do it.

What I Bring

  • Scale-minded architecture — every system assumes live revenue data with non-technical users who can't debug it. That's a global-user-base mindset.
  • LLM production discipline — hybrid architecture, audit trails, cost governance. I treat AI reliability as an engineering constraint.
  • Enterprise GTM depth — Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I understand the buyer and the stakeholder map.
  • Diverse user mindset — 50+ non-technical GTM users relying on systems I built. I design for the person who won't read the docs.
  • Independent builder — 12 active ventures, ROSTR framework published. I ship under real constraints with real accountability.
