Why Google.

I'm drawn to Google's depth across AI research, infrastructure, developer platforms, and global products. There's no other organization where I could learn how the strongest teams translate new model capabilities into dependable experiences at the scale of billions of users — and simultaneously access the infrastructure, research, and developer tooling to do it right. That combination is singular.

My production experience is built specifically on the translation problem: taking what a model can do and making it dependable enough that non-technical people can rely on it daily. At Atlas HXM, I built a two-channel AI inbound system that attributed $9M+ in pipeline with every dollar traceable. I built a compliance screening engine where the model does only the fuzzy work and deterministic code makes every auditable decision. I rebuilt a failing four-agent pipeline from root cause — a broken vector index — rather than patching symptoms.

The role I want at Google is Forward Deployed Engineer — in enterprise customer environments, translating Google's AI capability into real production systems, and closing the loop between deployment reality and product development. My 15-year enterprise sales background at Uber, Upwork, and Asana means I understand the customer's operating constraints, not just the technical spec. And I've built across the full stack — from agent architecture to CRM integration to attribution reporting — so I can do more than consult.

Google's infrastructure depth, research rigor, and commitment to responsible AI at scale are exactly the environment where I want to grow — and where I believe my applied production experience has the most to contribute.

What I Bring

  • Translation-layer expertise — I build the bridge between frontier model capability and what non-technical users can rely on daily. That's the gap Google has to close at global scale.
  • Production AI discipline — hybrid LLM + deterministic architecture, audit trails, cost governance, rollback plans. Designed for operations teams, not demos.
  • Enterprise GTM depth — Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I understand the customer's stakeholder map, not just the technical spec.
  • Global-scale mindset — Atlas HXM operates in 160+ countries. Every system I built had to work across that complexity. I'm used to thinking about edge cases at global scale.
  • Published framework — ROSTR is a citable, peer-reviewed methodology for production multi-agent AI. I can contribute to Google's developer ecosystem with demonstrated depth.
