Why Anthropic.

I'm drawn to Anthropic's uncompromising commitment to safety, interpretability, and constitutional AI. Most AI companies are racing to scale; Anthropic is racing to understand. That's the bar I want to work at.

My production work is built on a hybrid AI architecture: Claude handles what it's good at — reading messy input, semantic mapping, creative drafting — while deterministic, unit-tested code makes every consequential decision. I've shipped a compliance engine where Claude maps messy industry values to canonical sectors, then a JS rules engine makes the tier decision. The model never touches the final call. That's not philosophy; it's engineering discipline.

The role I want at Anthropic is Forward Deployed Engineer — inside enterprise customers' environments, helping them understand where Claude's capabilities end and where guardrails need to be, and building the bridge so they can deploy confidently. My enterprise background at Uber, Upwork, and Asana means I understand the customer's real constraints — not just the technical spec, but the regulatory, operational, and stakeholder map.

I also have a developer relations dimension worth naming. I've published the ROSTR framework for production multi-agent AI systems, built 40+ Claude skills for a 50-person GTM org, and I spend real time thinking about how to help other builders understand when Claude is the right choice and when to be careful. That's both a teaching interest and a credibility statement.

Anthropic is where the rigor is highest, where the questions are hardest, and where I can contribute my applied production experience to help others build AI systems worthy of trust.

What I Bring

  • Production AI discipline — hybrid LLM + deterministic architecture, audit trails, cost governance. Models don't make consequential calls; code does.
  • Enterprise GTM depth — Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I understand the customer's stakeholder map and communication style, not just tech specs.
  • Claude native practice — 40+ skills, 50+ daily non-technical users, real deployment constraints. I know where Claude shines and where it breaks.
  • Safety thinking — Every system I build ships with dry-run tests, rollback plans, and explicit failure modes documented. I design for the person who won't read the docs.
  • Published framework — ROSTR is a citable, peer-reviewed methodology for production multi-agent AI. Deployed in production across all systems in this portfolio.
