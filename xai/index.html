Why xAI.

I'm drawn to xAI's technical intensity and directness. I don't want to work near frontier AI — I want to work on closing the gap between what the model can do and what a production system can reliably deliver. xAI is moving faster than anyone else at that frontier, and that's exactly the environment where I want to grow.

I operate with high ownership. When I inherited a runaway enrichment spend problem, I didn't patch it — I redesigned the system around a free eligibility gate and tiered cost profiles, cutting spend per record by ~70% by architecture, not by asking people to be careful. When the post-call automation was at 0% pass rate, I diagnosed the root cause (a broken RAG vector index causing 84% of failures), removed it entirely, and rebuilt to a deterministic fetch. I own the problem until it's closed.

My production AI philosophy is deliberately constrained: LLMs do what they're good at — reading messy input, semantic mapping, drafting — and deterministic, unit-tested code makes every consequential decision. The compliance engine running on Atlas's live sales pipeline is the clearest example: the model maps a messy industry value to a canonical sector; a JS rules engine makes the tier decision. The model never makes the final call on a consequential output.

The role I'm targeting at xAI is Forward Deployed Engineer — being inside enterprise customers' environments, understanding exactly where the model hits its limits in real operating conditions, and building around those limits. My enterprise sales background at Uber, Upwork, and Asana means I understand what the customer's stakeholders actually care about.

xAI's technical intensity is exactly what I need to reach the next level. I want to work with people who hold the bar high because they respect the problem — not because they're performing rigor.

What I Bring

  • High-ownership bias — I own the runaway spend incident, the broken RAG architecture, the compliance bug caught on first live test. I document and fix, not blame and close.
  • Production AI discipline — hybrid LLM + deterministic architecture. Model makes no consequential calls. Everything auditable. Dry-run defaults, rollback plans, cost caps.
  • Speed without slop — I cut idea-to-scoped-project from 4–8 hours to under 30 minutes. 16 production systems in under two years at one company, plus 12 independent ventures.
  • Enterprise customer instincts — 15 years of sales at Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I find the real job behind the stated requirement and communicate the constraints clearly.
  • Real deployment experience — 50+ non-technical users relying on systems I built daily. I design for the person who won't read the docs and won't ask for help.
