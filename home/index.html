About

Fifteen years across entertainment, sales, and AI.

Patrick Diamitani owns AI strategy and automation across an entire go-to-market organization — sales, marketing, and revenue operations — at a global HR-tech (Employer of Record) platform with roughly 500 employees operating in 160+ countries. His mandate: build production systems that a non-technical team of 50+ reps and marketers can rely on daily, not demos that impress once and then quietly stop being used.

Four themes run through everything he builds. Hybrid AI architecture — LLMs handle what they're good at (reading messy text, drafting, classification), while deterministic, unit-tested code handles anything that must be auditable, like compliance tiers or money. Production discipline — every build ships with dry-run tests, rollback plans, and shared error handling. Cost engineering — AI and data-enrichment spend is measured and capped by design. Platform building — beyond any single automation, the real product is a reusable skill library, a project-intake system, and a build standard so the work compounds instead of resetting.

The result is a portfolio that reads less like a collection of scripts and more like a small internal product organization: an AI agent with its own attribution model, a compliance engine with full audit trails, a cost-governance framework that turned an owned mistake into a permanent safeguard, and the standards layer that let one person's work scale across fifty.

Approach

How I build.

Four principles that run through every system in the library.

Principle
Judgment vs. Interpretation

Hybrid AI Architecture

Language models handle what they're good at — reading unstructured text, mapping messy values to clean categories, drafting first-pass copy — while the actual decision (a compliance tier, a lifecycle stamp, a dollar figure) is made by deterministic, testable code that produces the same answer on the same input, every time.

Principle
Ship Safe, Fail Loud

Production Discipline

Nothing ships without a dry-run path, a rollback plan, and a way to fail loudly instead of silently. Systems that touch live CRM data default to report-only until a human signs off. Every automation shares a common error-handling pattern.

Principle
Cost by Architecture

Cost Engineering

AI and enrichment spend is treated as an engineering constraint, not an afterthought. Free eligibility checks run before any paid step, expensive operations are gated to the accounts that actually warrant them, and every workflow reports its own cost.

Principle
Work That Compounds

Platform Building

The most durable output isn't any single automation — it's the standard that makes the next one faster to build safely. A shared build methodology, a self-serve project-intake tool, and a reusable skill library mean the organization's AI capability compounds.
