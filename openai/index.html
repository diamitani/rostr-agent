Why OpenAI.

I want to close the final ten percent: learning how to ship AI products better, faster, stronger, and at real production scale. I want to take ambitious ideas from first principles to products that serve millions, create meaningful value, and earn trust. And I want to do that at the organization that defines what that bar looks like.

My path into AI was self-directed. In late 2023, I started with custom GPTs and automation tools — Make, n8n — then moved into AI-assisted web development and practical products for people in my life. That path taught me the difference between a compelling demo and a product: real systems require clear user value, reliable behavior, thoughtful safeguards, observability, cost discipline, and ownership when things break.

For the past year and a half, I've led GTM AI and automation work at Atlas HXM — building production systems used daily by 50+ non-technical revenue teammates at a 500-person, 160-country company. The builds include: a two-channel AI inbound system with $9M+ in attributed pipeline; a compliance screening engine where the LLM maps messy CRM data and a deterministic rules engine makes every auditable tier decision; a four-agent post-call pipeline I rebuilt from 0% pass rate after diagnosing a broken RAG index; a self-serve project-intake engine that cut scoping time from 4–8 hours to under 30 minutes.

The role I want at OpenAI is Forward Deployed Engineer — inside enterprise customers' environments, understanding where GPT-4o and the API hit their limits in real operating conditions, and building the bridge. My enterprise background at Uber, Upwork, and Asana means I understand the customer's stakeholder map and what "deployment" actually means to them.

OpenAI is where the bar is highest, the feedback loops are fastest, and the work matters most. That's exactly the environment I want.

What I Bring

  • Production AI from scratch — I started with the API in 2023 and have shipped 16 production systems since. No shortcuts, no inherited codebase.
  • OpenAI-native stack experience — Every production system uses Azure OpenAI (GPT-4, GPT-4o-mini). I understand governance, cost, and evaluation in live revenue pipelines.
  • Forward-deployed instincts — 50+ non-technical users on live CRM data with zero tolerance for wrong answers. That's the constraint that shaped every design decision.
  • Enterprise GTM depth — Uber, Upwork, Asana, Techstars. I find the real job behind the stated request and communicate constraints clearly.
  • Published framework — ROSTR is a citable, peer-reviewed methodology for production multi-agent AI. It's the intellectual layer under every system in this portfolio.
